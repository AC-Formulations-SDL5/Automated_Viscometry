    Discovery Mode

    Autonomous rheological analysis for materials of unknown viscosity. Step 1: smart RPM selection — the system probes each calibrated cell near the bulk offset (~0.35 mm above rough hit-point) until torque lands in the 25–35% window. Step 2: a full Z-height scan runs automatically at the discovered RPM (same pipeline as Perform Experiment).

    Only Z-calibrated cells may be selected. Washing runs between cells as in a normal experiment.

    Predicted Viscosity

    Rotational drag D = T%/RPM is fit at each spindle speed as:

    D(h) = A / (h + hc) + B

    with a universal geometric offset hc and silicone-oil calibration A = k·μp to recover apparent viscosity.

    Single RPM: Newtonian estimate (one η). Multiple RPMs: power-law flow curve with automatic regime classification (Newtonian, shear-thinning, shear-thickening).

    Points below the torque floor and deeper than the detected hitpoint (contact plateau) are excluded; the hitpoint and shallower in-gap points are kept. Sweeps with fit R² < 0.70 are rejected.
