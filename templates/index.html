    Predicted Viscosity

    Rotational drag D (torque % divided by spindle speed ω) follows a hyperbolic relationship with gap height h:

    (D/ω) = η·K / (h + h₀)

    Newtonian: D(h) = a / (h − b) (flow index n = 1).

    Non-Newtonian (shear-thinning): D(h) = a / |h − b|n with n < 1. The absolute value prevents complex results when n is fractional.

    The geometry constant K (scale factor MHYP = 2.330) maps the fitted coefficient a to viscosity: η (kCp) = |a| × MHYP.

    Points below the torque floor and at/after the detected hit point are excluded before a statistical middle-segment trim and curve_fit. Choose the mode under Design Experiment → Viscosity predictions.
